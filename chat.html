Why use a separate chat client?

The default Twitch chat works, but standalone clients give you a cleaner layout, better readability, stronger moderation tools, easier multi-channel usage, and better support for emotes. For larger chats, Chatty gives you more overview with the right settings. For smaller streamers, Chatterino7 is often comfortable enough for both chatting and moderating.

Choose your chat client

Chatty and Chatterino7 are both useful, but they fit different situations. Chatty is stronger for heavy moderation. Chatterino7 is better if you want a clean client for everyday chatting and lighter moderation.

Setup

Basic setup flow

A simple structure for explaining the setup without making the page too crowded.

Download a client

Use Chatty if you want the strongest moderation-focused setup, especially for larger chats. Use Chatterino7 if you want a modern chat client that is great for chatting and still comfortable for moderating smaller streams.

Log in with Twitch

Connect your Twitch account inside the client so you can chat, moderate, and access your normal Twitch permissions.

Join your channels

Add the channels you watch or moderate. For Chatterino7, use splits if you want several chats open at the same time.

Adjust highlights and filters

Set up mentions, highlight words, ignored phrases, and moderation filters so important messages are easier to notice. This matters especially in Chatty, where good settings can give you much more overview in larger chats.
